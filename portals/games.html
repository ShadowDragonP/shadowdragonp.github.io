Life as a Game Developer

I began the path of video game development in 8th grade, when my event in a state competition was to design a game in Python. In another similar competition, my task was to design a game in C# using the Unity3D engine. Neither games placed Top 3, but that was to be expected. After my first encounter with programming, I knew that I had to work harder in order to improve.

During the summer after 8th grade, I founded YummyFruits Studios, the sub-company that managed the programming sector of ShadowDragon Studios. In 9th grade, I teamed up with Matthew Duphily, a friend of mine whose programming expertise exceeded mine. Together, we developed games under YummyFruits Studios. In 2017, our game "FRUITS.EXE" placed third in Technology Student Association's (TSA) Video Game Design event.
